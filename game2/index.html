Door to Hell

  • Originality: This game is a multiplayer game with one player having point-and-click controls and another player with keyboard controls.
  • Technical Merit: Had to use tween to move a sprite to the mouse-click location in combination with a check to see if there should be a walking animation or not. Also, made a rudimentary system to keep track of game states of "play" and "end".
  • Prototype Postmortem: The most challenging part was initially figuring out how to combine a point-and-click experience with multiplayer capability. In addition to that, I had to figure out a way to use tween to move the sprite in a way that it would maintain relatively constant speed depending on a variable duration of the tween. I also learned how to activate animations and face the sprite in the correct direction.

  • High Concept: Door to Hell is a simple multiplayer mind game where player assumes the role of a human and another is Death itself.
  • Theme: The theme is semi-horror and death.
  • Mandated Variety:
    • Input: mouse to move and select options for player 1, arrow keys for player 2
  • Prototype Goal: This game was designed to prototype how to implement point-and-click movement and animation as well as multiplayer capability in a game.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to have fun playing a simple mind game.
  • Gameplay: Player 1: Use mouse to point-and-click for movement as well as selecting which door they want to go through.
    Player 2: Use the left and right arrow keys to secretly select a door to hide behind.
  • Strategies: Because the game is so simple, there aren't many strategies to implement in this game. However, players may need to think about the balance between taking damage from getting hit by a saw vs. collecting a diamond knowing that there will be chances to heal later. Also, the player may wish to stay near the center of the platform for easier access to the entire platform.
